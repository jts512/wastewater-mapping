Know Your Flow 🚽

Have you ever stopped to think about what happens after you flush?

Every day, millions of gallons of wastewater travel through New York City's sewer system to one of 14 wastewater resource recovery facilities. There, the water is cleaned through a multi-step process that removes solids, filters out pollutants, and uses bacteria to break down waste before being safely discharged back into local waterways.

The result: cleaner waterways across New York Harbor and beyond—clean enough that whales and dolphins are starting to make a comeback.
